# Quality Checklist

## Video Breakdown

- Real cut points and visible action changes are not merged away.
- Short actions such as touching, pulling, opening, turning, pressing, stretching, and arranging are captured.
- Each shot describes visible subject, action, product state, environment, composition, lighting, and texture.
- Captions or screen text are not treated as product package text.
- Unknown details are marked unclear instead of invented.

## Copy Extraction

- Audio transcription is checked first when the video has口播 or narration.
- If subtitles are present, high-resolution subtitle-area frames are reviewed, not only small contact sheets.
- The first 3 seconds are always manually/visually rechecked because hook wording changes the whole script framework.
- When audio and subtitle disagree, mark the conflict and prefer the clearer source; do not guess.
- Uncertain words are marked `听不清/看不清/待复核`, not silently rewritten.
- Final extracted copy is one sentence per line and contains no analysis.

## Replication Blueprint

- It filters shots into key storyboards instead of copying every detailed shot.
- It preserves hook, product first appearance, core visual proof, usage scene, specification/value, and closing.
- It merges or weakens repeated/low-value shots only when the conversion path remains intact.
- Each storyboard image is at most 12 cells and normally covers at most 15 seconds of source video.
- Benchmark videos around 15-17 seconds default to one compressed 15-second segment and one storyboard image; compression removes repeated holds or low-value transitions, not key conversion shots, and never adds or replaces shot content.
- No key storyboard crosses segment boundaries.
- No key storyboard introduces any product-reference-only shot absent from the benchmark video. The source video is the only authority for scene, action, prop, camera angle, background, and product presentation mode.

## Storyboard Prompts

- Output follows the blueprint's exact storyboard count, cell count, keyframe range, and time range.
- For replication runs, actual storyboard images are generated by default with Codex's built-in image generation tool. Do not stop at prompt text unless the user explicitly asks for prompts only.
- The actual generated image count equals the blueprint storyboard count.
- Do not generate only the first storyboard when the blueprint contains later storyboard segments.
- All storyboards are 9:16.
- Product fidelity is checked before visual attractiveness or composition similarity. If the product is wrong, deformed, missing required identity details, or not faithful to product images, the storyboard fails.
- Product images are declared highest priority and must lock category, color, shape, length/proportion, thickness, material, texture, seams/panels, logo/mark/text/pattern location, logo/mark/text/pattern size, logo/mark/text/pattern color, logo/mark/text/pattern direction, and category-specific details such as pendant, clasp, gemstone, connector, label, screen, button, cap, nozzle, packaging, or distinctive texture.
- Product identity details must not be removed to avoid rendering risk. If a logo/mark should be visible from the camera angle, it must remain visible in the physically correct place and scale.
- Product references lock appearance only; they are not permission to invent new shots. Do not add any scene, action, prop, camera angle, background, or product presentation mode unless the benchmark video contains that shot type.
- Later storyboard prompts request product images plus the first storyboard image.
- For multi-segment KOC/person storyboards, storyboard 2+ must be generated with the actual generated storyboard 1 as a visual reference. Text-only continuity instructions are insufficient for preserving the same person.
- If the generation tool cannot attach the actual first storyboard as an image reference, do not treat later person storyboards as final; mark them for regeneration in a tool that supports image references.
- Each cell is one complete image-generation description.
- Each cell obeys real physical-world logic: plausible scale, gravity, hand/object contact, body posture, product placement, lighting, reflections, and scene continuity.
- Logo-sensitive, text-sensitive, pattern-sensitive, and category-specific product details are checked as product identity, not optional decoration. For every cell, decide whether the relevant product surface is visible. If visible, the detail must stay on the correct physical area, rotate with the product surface, keep product-image scale/color/material, and avoid wrong places such as body, clothing, prop, floor, underside, wrong side, package, or background.
- If exact readable text is imperfect but the mark is present, physically placed, scaled, colored, and product images remain the Seedance appearance lock, note the risk. If the mark disappears or moves to the wrong physical location, fail the storyboard.

## Storyboard QC

Check every generated cell against the corresponding original frame/keyframe:

- Product fidelity: category, variant, color, shape, scale, length/proportion, thickness, texture, seams/panels, logo/mark/text/pattern, category-specific identity details, and no deformation.
- Identity-detail physical logic: should this angle show the relevant product detail, does it rotate/move with the product, is it the correct size/color/material/location, and is it absent only when naturally hidden.
- Real physical logic: contact points, gravity, support direction, hands/feet/body-part count, product count, fabric stretch, and no intersections.
- Benchmark similarity: framing, camera angle, product position, body-part position, hand action, prop relationship, scene structure, and shot function.
- Drift decision: accept, targeted regenerate, or fail.

Do not accept a generic product-ad cell that loses the benchmark shot function. Do not accept a benchmark-like cell with a wrong or deformed product.

## Retry Limit

- Generate the whole storyboard once and QC once.
- If only 1-2 cells have repairable issues, perform one targeted regeneration.
- If many cells drift, or a P0 product-fidelity failure remains after one targeted regeneration, stop and report failure reasons instead of continuing automatic retries.

## Seedance Video Prompts

- One prompt per storyboard segment.
- Each segment is 15 seconds or less.
- Prompts use concrete director language.
- Every cell/shot includes timecode, camera/framing, action, visible selling-point expression, scene, light, texture, and product consistency.
- Product images remain the highest appearance lock. If a generated storyboard cell has unclear identity detail, unclear logo/text, or minor product drift, the Seedance prompt must explicitly restore product appearance from the product images and use the storyboard only for composition, shot order, scene, light, and hand/body style.
- If the storyboard has missing/unclear/misplaced product identity details and the user asks to continue through Seedance API with their own configured API key, mark the storyboard as structure-only rather than passed. The prompt must explicitly say storyboard missing/wrong/misplaced product details are errors and must not be inherited.
- Every cell/shot with a logo/mark/text/pattern-sensitive or category-detail-sensitive product states whether the relevant product surface should be visible. If visible, specify correct physical location, size, color/material, direction, and surface rotation; if hidden, specify the physical reason such as angle, hand/body/prop cover, non-detail side, crop, or depth-of-field blur.
- Every cell/shot includes a physical-state contract when hands, feet, soft goods, jewelry, chains, or multiple product units move: contact points, support direction, object count before/after, visible body parts, and forbidden artifacts.
- No subtitles, screen text, or background music.
- No placeholder tokens such as `@图片X`, `@图片N+1`, `0:XX`, or `继续写完`.
- Do not reference generated video clips or video reference IDs; use only product image(s), current segment storyboard image, and optional first storyboard image for visual continuity.
- Actions obey real physical-world logic and do not include impossible hand positions, object intersections, product morphing, broken gravity, or discontinuous scene geography.
- High-risk continuous actions are simplified. If extraction, insertion, dressing, pulling, wrapping, or count changes could cause cloning/morphing, use a hard cut to a stable state rather than asking Seedance to simulate the whole action.
- Product count remains coherent: when one item is removed from a group, either cut to the single item only, or explicitly show the remaining group reduced by one; never let the same item appear twice.
- Body-part shots remain coherent: specify the exact number of hands, feet, legs, and visible support points; reject extra limbs, strange sleeves/pants, floating feet, and unclear contact.

## Final Delivery

- Default final user-facing deliverables are generated storyboard image file(s), rewritten imitation copy/script, and final Seedance video prompt(s).
- When API generation is requested and the user has configured their own API key, also deliver the downloaded MP4 and state whether final video product identity/logo QC passed or failed. If no key is configured, deliver the final prompts and reference-image order for manual Seedance generation instead.
- Internal analysis files are not part of the default final answer unless the user asks for full analysis, troubleshooting evidence, Feishu payload, or an archive.

## Cleanup

- Keep final storyboard images, rewritten imitation copy/script, final Seedance video prompts, and optional Feishu payloads.
- Delete extracted frame folders, subtitle/OCR review sheets, overview contact sheets, selected-frame review sheets, and temporary crops after the workflow is complete.
- Preserve temporary review files only when diagnosing a specific bad output or when the user explicitly asks to keep them.
